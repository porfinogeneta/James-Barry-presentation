Family Background

Barry was born as Margaret Anne Bulkley. Her parents were Jeremiah and Mary-Ann Bulkley.

How did she begun studies

Margaret soon came to the attention of very influential and progressive patrons, who saw in her a potential for learning and encouraged her to study medicine.

At the university

Barry studied at University of Edinburgh from 1809 to 1813 when he passed the examinations of the Royal College of Surgeons of England.

In the Cape Town

In 1815 James Barry joined the army and achieved a promotion to Assistant Surgeon of the Forces. Next year he was posted to Cape Town in South Africa, where got to know with Charles Henry Somerset (the governor of the Cape Colony).

His improvements of hygiene, water systems and the living conditions of the poor

Caesarean Section

In the middle of the June night in 1826 Barry was summoned to attend Wilhemina Monic with her childbirth. When Barry came it immediately appeared that the caesarean section would be necessary. Thanks to the high hygienic and techniques standards of Barry's work both the mother and the child survived.

During his life he put a lot of effort in maintaining and providing better hygiene and water systems to the poor.

What resulted in improvements of the living conditions of prisoners, slaves and mentally ill.

Death
